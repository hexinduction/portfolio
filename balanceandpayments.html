Case Study

Designing a balance and payments system for a 0-1 fintech product.

Overview

Quil (originally Quin) was founded to offer a safety net to people laid off from their job. It was the first unemployment benefit of its kind in the US, and the first fintech product to offer one.

The Problem

We needed a central balance and payments hub for membership fees and credit card activity.

Users

Initial audience - middle to middle upper income earning full-time employees.

Our intent was to eventually service all full-time workers regardless of employment status (contract, self-employed, etc.)

Role

Design Lead

Scope & Constraints

Timeline - 8 weeks for design and development.

Balance & Payments were essential features that needed to be included for launch. Quil was a banking product, but the core service resembled insurance, an industry with completely different regulations. It was necessary to avoid any language or imagery that would mislead consumers to believe that Quil was an insurance product.

Process

Define the problem

Our team, which included developers, design (me), and PMs, kicked off the project with a brainstorming session in Figjam. We identified our goals and user priorities.

Competitive Research

I reviewed the balance and payment features of various banking and fintech products, and membership-based services.

User flows and wireframes

I created user flows and wireframes to begin mapping outage ideas we captured in the brainstorm. I also pulled inspiration from my competitive research. I then shared these discoveries with the team, to make sure we were all in agreement. Next, our designs were reviewed by our banking partner’s legal team, who would request revisions or offer suggestions as needed.

Mockups

After reaching a consensus, I began designing hi fi mockups. I utilized brand guidelines as well as our burgeoning design system.

Outcomes & Results

The result was a strategically low-friction payment experience that empowered and benefited users. Our biggest accomplishment was that month-to-month users consistently made membership payments on time. Though we had a long-term strategy for obtaining feedback on the payment experience, it wasn’t implemented before Quin ultimately reformed as Quil due to a change in partnership.

Metrics

Quantitative

96%

On-time membersip fee payment.

0%

Requests for usability support using payments page.

Qualitative

Usability Feel

"There's not too much stuff."

- user research comment

UX with Aesthetics

Our CTO shared that a design lead at a large financial partner complimented the design for the simplicity and elegance of the layout.

Looking Forward

With a new partner and new name came the opportunity to redesign balance & payments utilizing a new design system. Though the new design system was not implemented, I continued to flesh it out based on user research and updated standards and patterns.
